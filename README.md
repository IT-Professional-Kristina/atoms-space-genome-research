## About This Paper

This paper traces the scientific chain from the 
historical discovery of atoms through atomic structure, 
molecular chemistry, DNA biology, chromosome organization, 
space radiation physics, and human disease.

It demonstrates that genomics is not only a biological 
discipline but a physical one — grounded in the behavior 
of matter, energy, and environment at the atomic scale.

Topics covered:
- Historical development of atomic theory
- Electron behavior and chemical bonding in DNA
- DNA double helix structure and chromosome organization
- Space radiation: galactic cosmic rays and solar particles
- Ionization, proton tunneling, and reactive oxygen species
- DNA damage: single-strand vs double-strand breaks
- Repair pathways: homologous recombination and NHEJ
- Chromosomal instability, genomic instability, disease risk
- Clinical informatics: genomic variant records in Epic EHR
